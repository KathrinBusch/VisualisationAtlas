Overview

Making sense of big data in movement ecology by interactive 3D visualizations

Christopher Monk, Marine Biologist

What does the graphic show?:

Fish can now be accurately tracked in high resolution in the wild, where positions can be collected every few seconds from hundreds of fish simultaneously over months to years. This is a screenshot from software developed to visualize high resolution animal movement tracks, interactively, in 3D and in context. This example shows common carp (Cyprinus carpio), tench (Tinca tinca), Eurasian perch (Perca fluviatilis) and European Catfish (Silurus glanis) tracked with acoustic telemetry in Kleiner DÃ¶llnsee, a research lake in north Brandenburg.

Personal perspective:

Converting millions of coordinates into an understanding of fish behavior requires time consuming detective work. By using this tool researchers can quickly observe and explore complex behaviours in their datasets and synthesize multiple datastreams.

How was this visualization created?:

This version was created with Unity Real-Time Development Platform, which is a free platform used for developing video games. A previous versions was also developed using Processing, and an in-browser version was written using observablehq.com
