Overview

Visualizing GLODAP – Three different routes

Nico Lange, Chemical Oceanographer (PhD-student)

What does the graphic show?:

The figure illustrates the anthropogenic carbon (Cant) concentrations normalized to the year 2002. Subplot a) shows the concentrations along a global section as indicated by the black line in subplot b). Subplot b) further shows the global Cant column inventory. The data used for the figure is taken from the mapped climatology of GLODAPv2 (GLobal Ocean Data Analysis Project, www.glodap.info), which has been created using high-quality and bias-corrected hydrographic cruise data and gridding it using DIVA.

Personal perspective:

The figure shown directly relates to GLODAP’s scientific rationale to help to determine the interior ocean’s capacity to store Cant. Very few calculation steps are needed to produce the figure as all data are already provided in a gridded format through GLODAP’s mapped climatology.

How was this visualization created?:

1. Download GLODAPv2 Mapped Climatology (netcdf files)

2. Calculate Cant inventory (integrate over layer depth; Matlab)

3. Create section(s) and global overview map (contourf.m; m_map; Matlab)
