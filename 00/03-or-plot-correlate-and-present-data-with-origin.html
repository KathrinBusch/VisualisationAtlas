Overview

Plot, correlate, and present data with Origin

Sophie Paul, Geochemist

What does the graphic show?:

The visualization shows an XY plot of the dissolved copper concentration [dCu] and the ligand concentration [L] at three nodule sites of the Pacific Ocean. Samples plotting above the 1:1 line indicate that L are in excess of dCu and that all dCu is presumably complexed (i.e. not free, not or less bioavailable).

Personal perspective:

For my research, simple scatter or line plots are sufficient because I have small amounts of trace element data that I easily want to graphically show. Integration with Excel is easy, in which I often do my data analysis first.

How was this visualization created?:

This plot was created using Origin. Origin is an easy-to-use interface spreadsheet software that helps to visualize data. The geochemical data from ICP-MS and voltammetry was first gathered in Excel and then copied to Origin to create the plot. Origin also offers many statistical analyses and can be linked with e.g., MATLAB, Python, and R for more advanced users.
