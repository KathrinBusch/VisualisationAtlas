Overview

Reproducible Visualization = Better Science

Armin Bernstetter, Computer Scientist, Doctoral Researcher

What does the graphic show?:

The image shows components of the “Digital Lab Book” web application, a framework supporting reproducibility, provenance and workflow documentation in visual data exploration.

- The Digital Earth Viewer, one of the visualization applications currently supported by the Digital Lab Book. The visualized data is a point cloud of an undersea volcano field including user annotations.

- Part of the main Digital Lab Book web application. A list of visualization states/events including screen shots, several user interface elements, and a list of the branches in the current project. The right side shows part of the current provenance graph/history.

- An exemplary mind map built in the mind map component of the Digital Lab Book resulting from an interaction session with the visualization seen in the left pannel).

Personal perspective:

The arrangement symbolizes working with a visualization software and having additional and independent third-party software in parallel, tracking and visualizing the interaction and other artifacts that are needed for better reproducibility.

How was this visualization created?:

Screenshotting, and arranging in PowerPoint (the image was originally used for a poster).
