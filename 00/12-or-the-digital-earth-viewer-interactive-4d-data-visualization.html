Overview

The Digital Earth Viewer – Interactive 4D Data Visualization

Valentin Buck & Flemming Staebler, computer scientist / software developer

What does the graphic show?:

The visualization, taken from the 2022 NASA Workshop “Viz4Climate”, shows wind currents across europe underlaid with a bathymetric terrain map. It is part of a series of visualizations that show how a regional release of methane into the atmosphere can lead to global consequences. For the workshop, we also provided an interactive instance.

Personal perspective:

Interactive visualizations allow users to recombine different components of the visualization (wind currents, methane concentration, release locations, etc.) and explore new viewpoints. Like all other artefacts of a publication, visualizations serve to convey information. Fixed visualizations can skew that conveyance in a certain direction. This can be used actively, to emphasize certain aspects of the data, but can also be caused by the passive biases and preconceptions of the creator of the visualization. While interactive visualizations still need to present some kind of initial scene or view into the data, the freedom to both figuratively and literally look behind the curtain can not only lead to a more whole understanding of the data, but also new insights.

How was this visualization created?:

The visualization was created using the Digital Earth Viewer, a 4D interactive visualization program created in the Digital Earth initiative at GEOMAR. For this example, the raw NetCDF-output files of the atmospheric simulation created at KIT were combined with high-resolution bathymetric data from GEBCO in the form of a GeoTiff and loaded directly into the viewer. From there, a scene could be created using the user interface that showed the bathymetric data as a shaded relief and would animate moving arrows to show the wind direction above it.

The program is open-source (EUPL v1.2) and available at https://git.geomar.de/digital-earth/digital-earth-viewer where you can also find a manual and video tutorials.
